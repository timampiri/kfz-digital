Can I register my car online if I only have a passport? +
Yes, if your passport has been registered with German authorities (Anmeldung) at your current address. The eID is required, but a passport works as an alternative for online identification.
My car was registered before 2015 — can I still register online? +
Older vehicles may not have the security codes needed for online registration. We recommend contacting our support team or visiting a local partner point to confirm eligibility.
What is an eVB number and how do I get one? +
An eVB (elektronische Versicherungsbestätigung) is a 7-digit code your insurance company provides confirming you have valid coverage. Call your insurer and ask for your eVB number — they'll provide it instantly.
How long does online registration actually take? +
Filling in your details takes 5–10 minutes. eID verification another 5 minutes. KBA processing takes 1–3 business days. You'll get confirmation via email as soon as it's approved.
Is it safe to send original documents to an online service? +
For fully online registration, you don't need to send original documents. We use digital verification (eID card + AusweisApp2) instead. For local partner registration, documents stay with the partner and are never shipped.
Why aren't prices shown here? +
Each partner sets their own service fee, and Bundesland authority fees vary. To keep prices accurate, the exact cost is shown in the first step of the online registration flow — or confirmed by your chosen partner after you contact them.

What is Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil I? +
Teil I is the vehicle registration certificate kept in your car. It's required for all registration types.
What is Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil II? +
Teil II is the title document (Fahrzeugbrief) kept at home. It proves ownership and is required for transfers and deregistration.
Where do I get an eVB number? +
Contact your insurance company. They'll provide your 7-digit eVB number instantly by phone, email, or their website.
Is HU/TÜV certificate always required? +
Yes, for all registration types. Your HU/TÜV must be current and not expired. For new vehicles, this may not yet be required.
What IBAN is acceptable? +
Any European IBAN is accepted. It's used for setting up the Kfz-Steuer (vehicle tax) direct debit. The account holder must match the registration holder.
Do I need original documents or copies? +
For online registration, originals are verified digitally. For local partner registration, bring originals to your appointment — copies are not accepted by authorities.
