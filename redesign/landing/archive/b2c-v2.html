Your two options

Which registration path is right for you?

Both options are fully legal and KBA-certified. The right choice depends on your situation — how fast you need it, how complex your case is, and whether you have a German eID card.

The process

How each option works, step by step

Two different paths, same outcome — a legally registered vehicle in Germany.

Local partner
1

Find a partner near you

Search by city or PLZ above. We show you the nearest certified partner point with opening hours and directions.

2

Prepare your documents

Zulassungsbescheinigung I & II, valid ID, eVB insurance number, and TÜV certificate. The partner will confirm your specific list.

3

Visit the partner office

Arrive with your documents. The partner processes your registration digitally via the KBA system — typically ~15 minutes.

4

Drive away

Registration confirmed digitally. You receive your updated Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil I. Plates can be arranged on the same visit.

Fully online
1

Check eligibility

You need a German Personalausweis with activated eID function, and your vehicle must have security codes in the Zulassungsbescheinigung.

2

Fill in your details

Complete the registration form with vehicle data, your eVB number, address, and bank account for Kfz-Steuer (vehicle tax) direct debit.

3

Verify with eID

Sign the application using your Personalausweis eID chip via the AusweisApp2 on your phone. Legally binding and secure.

4

Receive confirmation

The Zulassungsstelle processes your application — typically 1–3 business days. Digital confirmation sent by email when approved.

Frequently asked

No. The i-Kfz online portal requires a German Personalausweis (ID card) or an electronic Aufenthaltstitel (residence permit) with the eID online function activated. If you only have a passport or a non-electronic residence document, you'll need to use a local partner service instead.
Once you submit your application, the Zulassungsstelle processes it — typically within 1–3 business days. You'll receive confirmation by email when approved. Note: not all Zulassungsstellen have fully implemented the digital portal yet, so check availability for your district. At a local partner point, the entire process takes ~15 minutes on-site.
The German government sets lower official fees for i-Kfz online registrations to encourage digital uptake. Registering a new vehicle online costs €16.30, versus €30.60 at the counter. Deregistration online costs just €2.70, versus €16.80 at the office. The service fee to a local Zulassungsdienst (€150–200) is separate and covers their handling time, expertise, and KBA connection.
Probably not via the self-service online portal. Vehicles registered before 01.01.2015 generally don't have the required security codes in the Zulassungsbescheinigung needed for i-Kfz processing. However, a local partner service can still handle your registration — they have direct KBA access and can handle cases that the self-service portal cannot.
An eVB (elektronische Versicherungsbestätigung) is a 7-digit code from your car insurance provider confirming coverage. You cannot register a vehicle without it. Contact your insurer before your appointment — they'll provide the number electronically, usually within minutes of taking out a policy. German insurers like ADAC, HUK-Coburg, and others all issue eVB numbers as standard.
Reputable services like KfzPortal24 are official members of the KBA digitisation project and are legally accountable. That said, if you're uncomfortable sending original documents by post, a local partner service where you can hand them over in person may feel more secure. Both options are legitimate — it comes down to your personal preference.
