Documents you'll need.

Required for both options. Have these ready before you start.

01

Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil I

Vehicle registration document — the one you keep in the car.

02

Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil II

The title document — held by the owner or lender.

03

eVB number

7-digit electronic insurance confirmation from your insurer.

04

Valid ID (Personalausweis or passport)

With registered German address — Anmeldung must be in order.

05

HU / TÜV certificate

Current main inspection (Hauptuntersuchung) — valid, not expired.

06

IBAN (European bank account)

For vehicle tax (Kfz-Steuer) SEPA direct debit mandate.

How do I find my nearest partner?

Enter your postal code or city in the hero search, or tap one of the city chips. We show the three nearest partners with addresses, opening hours, distance, and a "Get directions" link. Most partners accept walk-ins; some offer same-day reservations through the page.

Walk in or do I need an appointment?

Walk-ins are accepted at every partner. If you'd rather not wait, most partners offer same-day slots — book directly from the partner card. The whole point is to avoid the multi-week appointment system at the Behörde.

What do I need to bring?

Bring your Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil I & II, a valid ID (Personalausweis or passport), an eVB number from your insurance, and your current TÜV/HU certificate. The partner verifies everything on the spot and helps if anything is missing.

How long will it actually take?

Median completion time at a partner is 14 minutes 32 seconds, measured across 50,000+ visits in 2025. Plates are stamped on the spot — you leave with everything you need to drive.

Can a partner handle complex cases?

Yes — imports, special configurations, name changes, and address transfers are all routine for partner staff. Cases the online flow can't handle (e.g. vehicles registered before 01.01.2015) are exactly what a human partner is best at.

My Zulassungsstelle is in a different city — does that matter?

No. Any Kennzeichen partner can register a vehicle for any Zulassungsstelle in Germany — the partner routes the submission through the official i-Kfz interface to your home registration authority automatically.

How does identity verification work online?

Two options. The German ID card (Personalausweis) with eID activated — quickest, ~2 minutes via the AusweisApp. Or video identification with a trained agent — ~5 minutes, available 7 days a week. Both are legally compliant under federal i-Kfz standards.

What if my eID isn't activated?

Use video identification instead — no eID needed. You'll need a smartphone or webcam, a valid ID document, and about 5 minutes of free time. The agent walks you through the steps in German or English.

When will I receive my plates?

Provisional registration arrives by email immediately upon completion — you can drive temporarily right away. Physical plates and stickers are delivered by DHL within 2–4 working days. Need them faster? Pick up at the nearest partner same-day.

Can I register any vehicle online?

Most new and used vehicles can be registered fully online. Edge cases — vehicles first registered before 01.01.2015 (missing security codes), some imports, and certain special configurations — are easier at a partner point. Our online flow will tell you upfront if your case isn't supported.

What if online verification fails?

Full refund — no questions asked. We'll also offer to route your case to the nearest partner point so you don't lose another evening. The money-back guarantee covers any technical failure on our side.

Is digital registration legally valid?

Yes — fully. Digital registration is regulated under the German Fahrzeug-Zulassungsverordnung (FZV) and the i-Kfz framework operated by the Kraftfahrt-Bundesamt. We're a recognised partner connected to the official i-Kfz interface, and your registration is legally identical to one done at a Zulassungsstelle.

What does registration actually cost?

Partner point: from €49.90, all-in. Online: from €99.90, all-in. Both prices include our service fee and all official Behördengebühren. No hidden charges, no upsells. What you see at checkout is the final amount.

Are Behördengebühren really included?

Yes. The official registration fees charged by the Zulassungsstelle are bundled into our flat price. You pay one amount to Kennzeichen, we settle the Behörde share on your behalf, and you receive a single tax-compliant invoice.

How does the money-back guarantee work?

If we can't complete your registration despite correct data on your side, you get a full refund — no questions asked. We'll also help route you to your nearest partner or Zulassungsstelle for free if a corner case requires the official office.

Which documents do I need?

Standard registration needs: Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil I & II, valid ID (Personalausweis or passport), eVB number from your insurer, and a current TÜV/HU certificate. SEPA mandate for the recurring Kfz tax. Imports and special cases may need additional papers — we'll flag exactly what's needed during the process.

How do I get an eVB number?

Your car insurance provider issues a 7-digit eVB number ("elektronische Versicherungsbestätigung"). You can usually request it online or by phone — most insurers respond within minutes. The eVB confirms your liability coverage and is mandatory for registration.

My TÜV/HU has expired — what now?

Get the TÜV/HU renewed first at any inspection point (TÜV, DEKRA, KÜS, GTÜ). Registration requires a valid certificate. A partner can often arrange same-day inspection in a nearby network if you ask when booking.
